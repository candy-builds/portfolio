Why Sponsor an H-1B1?
The H-1B1 provides U.S. employers a fast, predictable channel to hire Singaporean nationals in specialty roles—without the H-1B lottery, without a USCIS petition for consular applicants, and with a dedicated annual quota of 5,400 visas.
Cost Exemption for Employers:
The U.S. Embassy has confirmed that the new US$100,000 H-1B visa fee does not apply to H-1B1 visas available to Singaporeans. The standard application structure and baseline fees remain entirely unchanged.

Employer Sponsorship Process (Standard H-1B1 Stream)

  1. Extend a Job Offer: Offer a specialty occupation role requiring a bachelor's degree (or equivalent) in a specific field.
  2. File LCA (Form ETA-9035E): Submit Labor Condition Application to the Department of Labor (DOL) attesting to prevailing wage, work location, and no adverse effect on U.S. workers.
  3. LCA Certification (~7 working days): Once certified, maintain a Public Access File (PAF) at your place of business for inspection.
  4. Provide Documents to Employee: Give the employee the certified LCA, job offer letter, and employment contract.
  5. File Form I-129 (If Change of Status within U.S.): Request a Change of Status via USCIS. Premium processing takes 15 business days.

Alternative: Consular Path (Applying Outside the U.S.)

If the employee is in Singapore, skip the USCIS Form I-129 altogether:

  • DS-160: Employee completes the online visa application and schedules an interview at the U.S. Embassy in Singapore.
  • Consular Interview: Employee attends with the certified LCA, passport, job offer letter, and supporting credentials.
  • Visa Stamped: Upon approval, the visa is stamped and the employee enters the U.S. to begin work.

Candidate Status & Immediate H-1B Portability Onboarding
In addition to launching a standard H-1B1 process, I have a pending H-1B visa that takes official effect from October 2026 onwards. This grants your organization access to a highly efficient onboarding process known as H-1B Portability:
  • Immediate Employment Start: Under portability rules, an employee with an active/pending status can immediately begin to work at the new place of employment the moment the petitioner (the new employer) files the new or amended H-1B transfer petition with USCIS.
  • Zero Onboarding Latency: You do not need to wait for full USCIS petition approval or brand new visa stamping to bring me onto the team; the legal act of filing the transfer documents instantly clears me to start work.
